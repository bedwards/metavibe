Starship, Artemis, and the New Space Race | December 2025

SpaceX broke its single-year launch record in 2025 with 170 launches—165 Falcon 9 flights and 5 Starship suborbital tests. By October 13, Starship had launched 11 times total with 6 successful flights. NASA marked significant progress toward Artemis II, the first crewed mission around the Moon in over 50 years. China is targeting its own crewed lunar landing by 2030. The new space race is accelerating.

SpaceX Starship

2025 Flight Record

As of October 2025, Starship has launched 11 times total, with 6 successful flights and 5 failures. The first three 2025 flights were checkered—at least one stage lost on each. But the final two (August and October) were fully successful:

  • Super Heavy booster returned for pinpoint touchdown in the Gulf of Mexico
  • Upper stage reached space, deployed dummy Starlink satellites
  • Controlled splashdown in the Indian Ocean

SpaceX had hoped to catch a Starship upper stage in 2025, targeting as many as 25 launches. This didn't occur—only 5 flights by mid-October, with no additional launches scheduled for the remaining months.

Technical Specifications

Starship is the largest and most powerful rocket ever built:

  • Height: 121 meters (397 feet) fully stacked
  • Thrust: 74.4 MN (16.7 million pounds) from 33 Raptor engines
  • Payload to LEO: 100-150+ metric tons (fully reusable)
  • Propellant: Liquid methane and liquid oxygen
The Starship Human Landing System is scheduled to deliver astronauts to the Moon as part of Artemis III, currently targeted for 2027.

Artemis Program

Mission Timeline

  • Artemis II (April 2026) - First crewed flight beyond the Moon since Apollo 17
  • Artemis III (Mid-2027) - First crewed lunar landing since 1972, using SpaceX Starship HLS
  • Artemis IV (September 2028) - Delivers Gateway's core module
  • Artemis V (March 2030) - Uses Blue Origin's Blue Moon lander

SpaceX Lunar Contracts

  • April 2021: $2.89 billion fixed-price contract for Artemis III Starship HLS
  • 2022: $1.15 billion fixed-price contract for Artemis IV lander
  • 2025: SpaceX has completed 49 milestones for the Artemis lander

SpaceX is working two parallel Starship paths: the "core" megarocket (self-funded, targeting Mars) and the Artemis-specific moon-lander upper stage (NASA-funded). SpaceX is exploring a "simplified" Artemis III mission to accelerate the lunar landing timeline.

Global Competition

China's Lunar Program

China is targeting a crewed lunar landing by 2030, adding urgency to NASA's program. China's Chang'e missions have already demonstrated lunar sample return capability (Chang'e 5, 2020) and landed on the lunar far side (Chang'e 4, 2019).

Private Moon Landings

2025 saw the first successful private Moon landing, marking a new era of commercial lunar access. Multiple companies are now developing lunar lander capabilities.

2025 Mission Highlights

  • NASA Europa Clipper - Launched October 2024, en route to Jupiter's moon Europa
  • JAXA Martian Moons eXploration (MMX) - Targeting Phobos sample return
  • SpaceX Falcon 9 - 165 launches in 2025, setting new record
  • Blue Origin New Glenn - First orbital flight attempts

Why Space Shares

Public funding. NASA, ESA, JAXA—major space agencies are government-funded and publish openly. Taxpayer-funded research is publicly accessible.

International collaboration. The International Space Station demonstrates that space exploration transcends borders. Artemis includes partners from ESA, JAXA, CSA, and commercial providers.

Competitive pressure. SpaceX's transparency about Starship development—streaming launches, publishing trajectories—forces competitors toward openness. Blue Origin and others follow suit.

The result: more accessible space data than ever. Launch telemetry streams live. Mission archives go online within weeks. The race to the Moon plays out in public view.

The Battery Revolution and Superconductor Quest | December 2025

On April 21, 2025, CATL launched the world's first mass-produced sodium-ion battery—the Naxtra—with 10,000+ cycle life and 25-year durability. In December, researchers at the Max Planck Institute solved a decades-old mystery about high-temperature superconductors, directly measuring the superconducting gap in hydrogen sulfide for the first time. A new topological superconductor, PtBi2, was discovered with surface-only superconductivity that naturally hosts Majorana particles. The materials science breakthroughs of 2025 are reshaping both energy storage and quantum computing.

Sodium-Ion Batteries: The Lithium Alternative

CATL Naxtra

CATL's Naxtra sodium-ion battery represents a milestone in commercialization:

  • Energy density: 175 Wh/kg
  • Cycle life: 10,000+ cycles (some prototypes hit 20,000 while retaining 70% capacity)
  • Temperature range: -40°C to 70°C with 90% energy retention at -40°C
  • Charging: 80% in 15 minutes
  • Range: 500 km (310 miles)
  • Durability: 25+ years under normal use

Sodium is 1,000+ times more abundant than lithium. Production costs: $10-30/kWh—70-90% cheaper than lithium-ion. By 2027, Chinese manufacturers expect sodium-ion costs to reach $0.04/kWh, comparable to LFP.

BYD Grid Storage

BYD broke ground on a 30 GWh sodium-ion factory in January 2024 and launched the MC Cube-SIB containerized storage system for utilities. In March 2025, BYD confirmed investments targeting cost parity with lithium-iron-phosphate batteries, claiming system costs below $0.03/kWh.

Market Scale

CATL and BYD hold over 50% of the world's EV battery market share (811.7 GWh from January-September 2025). China's sodium-ion market is projected to grow from 10 GWh in 2025 to 292 GWh by 2034—45% annual growth. By 2030, China is expected to account for over 90% of global sodium-ion production.

A 20 GWh sodium-ion manufacturing plant was announced in China in November 2025.

The word in China is that sodium-ion is having a "DeepSeek moment"—lasts 25 years for a tenth of the price.

Superconductor Breakthroughs

Hydrogen Sulfide Mystery Solved

Researchers at the Max Planck Institute developed planar electron tunneling spectroscopy that operates under extreme pressures, directly probing the superconducting gap in H3S for the first time. This confirms how electrons pair so efficiently in high-temperature superconductors. Hydrogen sulfide becomes superconducting at 203K (-70°C); lanthanum decahydride reaches 250K (-23°C).

PtBi2: A New Topological Superconductor

Platinum-bismuth-two (PtBi2) exhibits unprecedented behavior: only its top and bottom surfaces become superconducting. The edges surrounding these superconducting surfaces naturally host Majorana particles—promising building blocks for fault-tolerant quantum bits. This discovery provides a new pathway for topological quantum computing.

Room-Pressure Nickelate Superconductors

At SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory, researchers stabilized a promising new class of high-temperature superconductors at room pressure—a significant step toward practical applications like lossless power grids and quantum technologies.

Theoretical Framework

Penn State scientists devised a new method to predict superconducting materials using "zentropy theory," bridging classical superconductivity theory with quantum mechanics. Theoretical physicists revealed that room-temperature superconductivity is possible within the laws of physics, linked to fundamental constants like electron mass and Planck's constant.

Prof. Olle Eriksson, Chair of the Nobel Prize Committee for Physics, stated: "This discovery will attract considerable scientific interest."

Solid-State Batteries

Scientists created new solid-state sodium-ion batteries that could make EVs cheaper and safer. Unlike liquid-electrolyte batteries, solid-state designs eliminate fire risk and enable higher energy density. The 2025 Solid-State & Sodium-Ion Battery Summit highlighted convergence between these technologies.

Challenges

Current lithium prices in 2025 are at historic lows, diminishing sodium-ion's cost advantage. When lithium prices spiked in 2022, excitement surged around sodium alternatives. With lithium prices fallen, sodium's advantage isn't as pronounced.

Natron Energy, a once high-flying sodium-ion startup, collapsed in September 2025, barely a year after announcing plans for a $1.4 billion, 14-GW manufacturing facility in North Carolina. The U.S. sodium-ion ecosystem remains fragile while China dominates.

Why Materials Science Shares

Reproducibility. Materials discoveries require verification across labs. Open data enables independent confirmation of crystal structures and properties.

Simulation. The Materials Project hosts computed properties for 150,000+ materials. AI models trained on shared data predict new materials before synthesis.

Industrial scale. Battery technology moves from lab to gigafactory. Open specifications accelerate manufacturing scale-up—CATL's Naxtra went from announcement to mass production in months because the underlying sodium-ion chemistry was well-documented.
